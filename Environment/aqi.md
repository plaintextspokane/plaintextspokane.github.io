**Checking AQI**

AQI stands for Air Quality Index.  
A higher the AQI number means a higher the level of air pollution & health risks. 

|Number| Color | Concern|
|:---|:---|:---|
|0 to 50|Green|Good|
|51 to 100|Yellow| Moderate|
|101 - 150|Orange|Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups|
|151 - 200|Red|Unhealthy|
|201 - 300|Purple|Very Unhealthy|
|301 and higher|Maroon|Hazardous|  

There are various ways to check the AQI in Spokane.

1. Check local weather and Air Quality Index (AQI) daily during wildfire season.
2. Locally, the Spokane Regional Clean Air Agency is a great resource for current and forecasted conditions:
  <a href="https://www.spokanecleanair.org/" target="_blank">Spokane Regional Clean Air agency ↑</a>

3. A national resource for more information is <a href="https://www.airnow.gov" target="_blank">Airnow.gov ↑</a>
