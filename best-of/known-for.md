Title: Known for
---

**Spokanites are proud to be known for many things**

* Nature's beauty is found all around and throughtout Spokane. The <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riverfront_Park_(Spokane,_Washington)#/media/File:Upper_Spokane_Falls_20080524.jpg" target="_blank">river ↑<a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spokane_Falls#/media/File:Spokane_lower_falls.jpg" target="_blank">falls ↑</a> downtown are a site to behold.
* Spotlightighting the Spokane river running through the town, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riverfront_Park_(Spokane,_Washington)" target="_blank">Riverfront Park ↑</a> is a source of local pride. Orginally the site of the city's railroad history, the downtown space underwent extensive renewal for the 1974 World's Fair.  
* The city's other signature park, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manito_Park" target="_blank">Manito Park and Botanical Gardens ↑<a/>, is a Registered Historic Place and a 90-acre park nesteld into the South Hill neighborhoods.
* Nationally, the city is known as the home to Gonzaga University and its storied NCAA basketball team, the Bulldogs.
* Also in the world of basketball, Spokane hosts the world's largest 3-on-3 basketball tournament every year, known as Hoopfest.
* The city is also home to a unique annual run known as Bloomsday - a 12 km run through the heart of the city - that attracts runners from around the state and the world.
